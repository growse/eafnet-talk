The Internet Protocol (IP)
* Quick refresher course on IP * Numbering scheme for nodes in a network. * A network is a collection of things that are connected together somehow * give every node a unique number, or address. * Postal address analogy. Give everything a unique address, and then you can send stuff to places * So what does an IP address look like?
1
Well, this is an address. It's the number "1".
42541014265448851428341158021258918306
* Another address * Simple scheme, give everything a number * Computers like numbers, especially fixed-width. Bad at variable width. * We decided IPs should be 128-bits * Old, experimental version of the internet that uses 32 * 128 gives us a lot. How many?
3.4 x 1038
* 340 trillion trillion trillion. * Big numbers hard to reason about. 1% is atoms on earth surface. 4x10^15 addresses for each star in the universe * Each address just 16 bytes. * How de we create a hierarchy? Like other addressing schemes (postal)
2001:19f0:7400:8287:5400:04ff:fe29:1f39
* 16 bytes becomes 8 groups of 2 bytes. * each 2 byte (or 16 bit) group can be displayed as two hex chars * Put a colon between each group * Idea is that you read from less specific to more, left-to-right * This is the same address as that big long address I showed before * What does "1" look like?
0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0000:0001
* Bit clumsy * Take leading zeros off each group, collapse adjacent groups of all zeros
::1
* Much better * Who knows what this is?
2001:678:b0c::/48
* Final bit, need a notation for a block of addresses (subnet) * This means left-most 48 bits (3 groups of 16 bits) are 2001:678:b0c * The bigger this slash number, the *fewer* addresses in the block, getting more specific * Who owns and decides who gets to control a subnet?

What is the internet?
* Bunch of routers connected together that know how to route IP packets to a destination
What is a router?
* What is a router? Device that receives packets and works out where to send them. List of routes in a routing table * Packets have a destination address, router matches to a subnet it knows how to route, sends it to that destination * The internet is basically lots of routers linked together. * You probably have one in your house * Let's see an example

Border Gateway Protocol
* BGP! * Old protocol, basically used for routers to advertise routes to other routers. * Routers have many routes, fun features like metrics, loop detection * Constant flux and chatter * Source of many hilarious outages (Facebook, tiny ISP deciding to route all of youtube through itself etc.)
AS204946
* BGP uses concept of Autonomous system * Want to advertise routes? Need an AS. * Got mine from RIPE along with my subnet. * About 31.5k AS's active, of which 20k are only publishing 1 prefix * To advertise, I need to find a router that'll accept a BGP advert. * Could maybe use my home internet connection
* Actually easier to find a friendly hosting company * Vultr have lots of locations, and are cheap

Anycasting
* Having multiple up is called Anycasting * common technique of CDNs to optimize content delivery * Routing rules means that clients will hit their "closest" endpoint * Also, good resilience * Gotchas include tying service availability to route publishing
